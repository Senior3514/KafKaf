# Code of Conduct

KafKaf exists so that running a private, capable AI doesn't require
handing your data to anyone or paying for the privilege. That same spirit
— useful to as many people as possible, without gatekeeping — applies to
the project itself.

## The short version

Be respectful. Disagree about code, architecture, and ideas as much as
you want — that's how the project gets better. Don't make it personal,
don't harass anyone, and don't make KafKaf's spaces (issues, PRs,
discussions) unwelcoming for anyone based on who they are.

## Expected

- Give and receive technical criticism without it becoming personal.
- Assume good faith, especially across language barriers — this project's
  own history includes plenty of clarifying back-and-forth, in more than
  one language, before landing on the right design.
- Credit others' ideas and contributions accurately.
- Respect that maintainers' time is limited; a clear bug report or a
  focused PR (see `CONTRIBUTING.md`) gets far more attention than a vague
  one.

## Not okay

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks, in any project space.
- Discriminatory language or behavior related to identity — including but
  not limited to race, gender, religion, nationality, sexual orientation,
  disability, or age.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Deliberately submitting malicious code, or using project spaces to
  promote unrelated products, scams, or harassment campaigns.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces — the repository (issues, PRs,
discussions, commits), and any other official KafKaf community space, as
well as anyone representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If something happens that violates this, open an issue if you're
comfortable doing so publicly, or contact a maintainer directly for
anything sensitive. Reports are handled with discretion. Maintainers who
don't follow their own standard are held to it too.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions, comments, or
participation that don't align with this Code of Conduct, and may
temporarily or permanently restrict access to project spaces for behavior
they deem inappropriate, threatening, or harmful — proportionate to the
issue, with an explanation where reasonable.

---

Adapted for KafKaf's own context; the enforcement structure follows the
approach used by many open-source projects under the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/).
